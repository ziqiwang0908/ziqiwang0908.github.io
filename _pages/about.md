I’m Ziqi Wang, a Master’s student in Computer Science at [Rutgers University](https://www.rutgers.edu/), graduating in January 2027. My research focuses on improving AI architectures, with a specific interest in the attention mechanism and how information flows in large models.

Currently, I am working with [Prof. Xiao Luo](https://luoxiao12.github.io/)(UW-Madison) on AURORA, a project that uses attention masking to help LLM agents become more reliable. I was also a member of [Prof. Yongfeng Zhang's](https://yongfeng.me/) lab at Rutgers, where I developed RAGRouter-Bench to analyze how embedding space properties affect RAG performance.

This semester, I am the Teaching Assistant for [Prof. Wes Cowan](https://sites.rutgers.edu/wes-cowan/) in [CS501: Mathematical Foundations of Data Science](https://sites.rutgers.edu/wes-cowan/cs-501-mathematical-foundations-of-computer-science/). I enjoy exploring the mathematical side of machine learning and am always open to new research collaborations.

## Research Interest
* Foundation Model Architectures (Transformer & Attention), Representation Learning & Embedding Analysis, Reliable & Trustworthy AI Agents
